How did I end up here?

It all started way back in 2014, I got my first touchscreen phone. I was 10. Obviously the first thing I tried was installing games. All my friends were on Dream League Soccer 2014, and I wanted in. Hit install on Google Play, boom – error. No space. My Sony Xperia Tipo Dual had 4GB of storage, not enough for the game + OBB cache unpacking. Even the SD card wasn’t helping.

Found a random guide online saying I could just download the OBB cache manually and unpack it into /Android/obb. At 10, I couldn’t believe something like that was real. Just... do that and it works? No way. Took me a week to build up courage. But I did it. Got zArchiver, unzipped that file into the right folder, not believing it would work — and boom, miracle, it launched.

And that was my intro to the file system and realizing I know nothing (yeah yeah Socrates). The actual issue was Google Play needed 2.5x space to unpack + store the same cache. A year later, I flashed CWM recovery and leveled up from Android 4.0.4 to 4.4.0. Then I got into custom ROMs, kernels, and trying to stretch compatibility between my ancient phone and new games. Felt great unlocking installs that "shouldn’t" be possible.

Then came 2016, my parents banned me from using the laptop. Forgot to take out the trash. They took away the charger. I had 2 hours of battery per day. Problem solved easily — a friend had the same charger. I started sneaking it and hiding it. Parents suspected nothing… until I got a little *too* happy. Next step? Password on the laptop.

I stared at that damn login screen for two weeks. First attempt: flour on the keys and asking mom to type the password (I *did* turn away). Didn't work long-term, they changed it. So I made a bootable flash drive with Rescue Setup, added a new user account while “on allowed time”, and boom — they thought I was locked out, but I was vibing.

Lotta folks been through similar phases. Wifite2 on Kali Nethunter, grabbing handshakes, intercepting parental logins on old HTTP Facebook via Interceptor-ng… that’s how it all began.
